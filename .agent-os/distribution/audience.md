# ResumeBuilder — Audience

## Segments

### Segment A: Active job seeker, English

- Trigger: applying to multiple roles per week
- Lives on: LinkedIn, job boards, r/jobs, r/resumes
- Pains: every role needs a different resume, ATS rejection fear
- Workarounds: Google Docs templates, paid resume services, ChatGPT prompts
- Words they use: "ATS resume", "tailor my resume", "resume template", "applicant tracking"

### Segment B: Career switcher

- Trigger: pivoting industries or roles
- Lives on: LinkedIn, Reddit career subs, career-coach newsletters
- Pains: how to translate prior experience to a new function
- Workarounds: a career coach, copying templates from new field
- Words they use: "career change resume", "career pivot", "transferable skills"

### Segment C: Polisher (existing resume, specific role)

- Trigger: applying for one or two important roles
- Lives on: LinkedIn, professional networks
- Pains: existing resume feels stale; not sure what to cut
- Workarounds: ask a friend in the industry, hire someone
- Words they use: "improve my resume", "make my resume stronger"

### Segment D: Hebrew-speaking job seeker

- Trigger: applying in the Israeli market
- Lives on: LinkedIn (he/en), Israeli job boards
- Pains: most resume tools assume US English conventions
- Workarounds: Word templates, ad hoc help
- Words they use: (Hebrew equivalents and Hebrew job titles)

## Anti-Segments

- Senior executives wanting bespoke human help — refer out
- Students with no experience — usable but not core ICP
- Recruiters / TA professionals — not the buyer
