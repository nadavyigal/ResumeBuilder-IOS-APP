# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-06-28*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Resumely helps job seekers check whether a role is worth applying to, tailor their resume to that job, and export a complete application package from their iPhone.
**What it does:** Users upload a resume, add a job description or job link, see a self-defined Resumely Match Score and fit guidance, apply targeted edits, choose a professional template, and export a polished resume PDF. The app also helps assemble a cover letter and job-specific application package.
**Product category:** AI resume tailor, resume optimizer, job match checker, resume builder, ATS-friendly resume tool.
**Product type:** Native iOS app with supporting web/backend services.
**Business model:** Free activation path while v1.2 validates upload, fit-check, optimization, and export activation. Monetization, paid acquisition, and paywall timing stay deferred until post-1.2 funnel data is readable.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** B2C, not company-targeted.
**Decision-makers:** Individual job seekers.
**Primary use case:** A job seeker wants to apply to a specific role and needs to know what is missing, what to improve, and how to submit a stronger package from mobile.
**Jobs to be done:**
- Check whether this job is a strong, stretch, or poor fit before investing effort.
- Tailor an existing resume to a specific posting without losing the user's real voice.
- Export a polished resume, cover letter, and application package from a phone.
**Use cases:**
- Upload a PDF or Word resume, add a job link/description, and run the Fit-First flow.
- Review missing keywords, top gaps, and a match score before optimizing.
- Apply targeted edits, keep facts under control, and export a recruiter-ready package.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Job seekers waste effort on generic applications and roles that may not be a realistic fit, while worrying that their resume is missing the right signals.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Generic resume builders focus on templates, not the specific job.
- ATS-score tools often imply an official score that no employer actually provides.
- AI rewrite tools can create generic, over-polished copy that does not sound like the candidate.
- Desktop-heavy job-search tools do not match mobile-first application behavior.
**What it costs them:** Time, missed applications, weaker submissions, repeated manual rewriting, and uncertainty before applying.
**Emotional tension:** "Is this role worth my effort?", "What am I missing?", "Will this sound fake?", "Can I submit this confidently?"

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Teal, Jobscan, Rezi, Kickresume, Resume Worded, Enhancv — often strong on resume scoring/building, but either desktop-heavy, generic, or centered on the score rather than the mobile application package.
**Secondary:** ChatGPT or other AI writing tools — flexible but unstructured, easy to over-genericize, and not tied to a polished export flow.
**Indirect:** Manual editing in Google Docs/Word or hiring a resume writer — familiar but slow, inconsistent, and hard to repeat per job.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Fit-First triage: Strong / Stretch / Skip before full optimization.
- Self-defined Resumely Match Score framed as job fit guidance, not an employer ATS verdict.
- Mobile-native path from upload to fit check to edits to application package.
- User-controlled, targeted edits rather than black-box rewrites.
- English + Hebrew / RTL support.
**How we do it differently:** Resumely starts with whether the job is worth pursuing, then turns the gap list into specific edits and a complete package.
**Why that's better:** It helps users apply to fewer, better-fit jobs instead of spraying generic resumes.
**Why customers choose us:** They want a fast, honest, mobile-first way to know what to fix and send a stronger application.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Can any app really know my ATS score?" | Resumely does not claim to show an official employer ATS score. The score is Resumely's estimate of resume-to-job fit, based on formatting and keyword match signals. |
| "Will AI make my resume sound fake?" | Resumely is positioned around targeted, user-controlled edits and keeping facts under the user's control. |
| "I do not want another subscription before I know it works." | v1.2 focuses on free activation and readable funnel data before paid acquisition or aggressive monetization. |

**Anti-persona:** People looking for a job board, auto-apply bot, guaranteed interview shortcut, or a from-scratch desktop resume design suite.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Generic resumes, repetitive rewriting, uncertainty about job fit, and mobile friction in existing tools.
**Pull:** Instant fit guidance, targeted edits, polished export, cover letter/application package, and mobile-native completion.
**Habit:** Users already use Word/Google Docs, ChatGPT, or one generic resume for many jobs.
**Anxiety:** They may worry about uploading a resume, trusting AI wording, or whether a score is misleading.

## Approved Marketing Execution Rules
- ASO should lead with Fit/Match and complete application package, not "pass ATS."
- Keep "ATS" for discoverability only in process-descriptive contexts such as ATS-friendly formatting or common parsing checks.
- Do not run paid acquisition until v1.2 post-live funnel data is readable.
- Fit-First is the front-door story: check fit, see what is missing, then tailor and export.
- Promotional Text for 1.2 (7): "Check your fit before you apply. Resumely shows what is missing, helps tailor your resume, and exports a complete application package from your iPhone."
